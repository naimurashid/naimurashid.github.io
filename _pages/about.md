I am an assistant professor in the Department of Biostatistics at the Gillings School of Global Public Health at UNC-CH, and have a joint appointment at the Lineberger Comprehensive Cancer Center. 

In addition to performing methodological research in genomics and machine learning, I also engage in collaborative work at the Lineberger Comprehensive Cancer Center, working with physicians and researchers on problems relating to genomics and clinical studies.  

I also work with oncologists to design cancer clinical trials and am on a number of active protocols locally at UNC and elsewhere. My work as part of the TBCRC Statistical Working Group also allows me to assist breast cancer researchers nationwide in the development and review of novel clinical trials in breast cancer. 

Each spring I teach a course each spring on Statistical Computing (BIOS 735) for PhD Students at Gillings, covering topics such R programming, various statistical and computational algorithms, and machine learning models. 


Research Interests
===================

My methodological work spans several areas in genomics and machine learning, including

* Flexible analysis of epigenomic activity from diverse types of highthrouput sequencing experiments, such as ChIP-seq, FAIRE-seq, DNase-seq, and related experiments
* Model-based unsupervised clustering and feature selection of RNA-seq datasets, adjusting for confounding variables
* Multi-study replicability of clinical prediction models 
* Accounting for RNA-seq transcript quantification uncertainty in detecting differential transcript usage events

Many of these projects are motivated by problems found during my collaborative work at Lineberger.
